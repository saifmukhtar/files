# Emergent Ventures Interview Preparation

## 1. Tell us about yourself

**Answer:** My name is Saif. I am an independent researcher from India working on a computational theoretical physics project called HCSN, which stands for Hierarchical Closure Structure Network. My work explores whether stable particle-like structures and interaction patterns can emerge from simple local rewrite rules on a directed hypergraph, without assuming spacetime or particles at the start.

I began this project from a deep curiosity about one question: why does stable matter exist at all? Over time, I taught myself the tools I needed, improved the simulation engine significantly, and kept refining both the technical and scientific side of the project.

What motivates me most is the possibility of discovering something fundamental, while being honest about what is still uncertain and what still needs testing.

---

## 2. What is HCSN in simple terms?

**Answer:** HCSN is a simulation-based research project that studies whether complex physical behavior can emerge from very simple local graph rewrite rules.

The core idea is to start with a directed hypergraph instead of assuming particles, fields, or spacetime. The graph evolves through local rewrites, and I study whether stable, localized structures can form and persist.

In simple terms: I am testing whether something like particle behavior can arise naturally from deeper discrete dynamics.

I see the current results as promising simulation evidence, not proof of new physics. My focus is on careful testing, improving robustness, and understanding what the system is actually doing.
